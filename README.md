# Intelligent-Cluster-Head-Selection-for-WSNs-Using-Modified-Particle-Swarm-Optimization



Wireless Sensor Networks (WSNs) play a crucial
role in various scientific and industrial applications; however,
energy efficiency remains a key challenge affecting sensor node
performance and overall network lifespan. Cluster-based hierar
chies are commonly employed to optimize energy consumption,
with cluster head (CH) selection being a critical factor. Recently,
metaheuristic optimization techniques have been leveraged to
enhance CH selection, improving network efficiency. This re
search proposes a modified particle swarm optimization (PSO)
algorithm for CH selection to extend network lifetime and
enhance energy efficiency. The proposed algorithm is evaluated
through simulations and compared with established optimization
techniques. Results indicate significant improvements in residual
energy, the number of active nodes, and convergence speed,
demonstrating the effectiveness of the modified PSO in energy
efficient WSN management
